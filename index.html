◉ Occurio


OCCURIO is a hi-fi prototype designed as a senior project for my UXD course at university. Built while learning the basic principles of UXD, Occurio is an application front-end that allows students to easily find, share, and list events happening around campus, whether they be hosted by students, student leaders, or faculty.

Aiming for an informed and fluid design, 2 other students and I used interview results, experience maps, iconography guides, and many, MANY whiteboard drawings to make the most appropriate design choices. Within the group, my primary responsibility was translating our rough design ideas into concrete wireframes, visual guides, and eventually the final prototype.

I learned a lot about the UXD process from this project, and found it helped me to pinpoint my personal strengths as a graphic designer in a digital medium.

◉ Soundsky

SOUNDSKY is a simple music-streaming mockup, meant to be a study in how to design an interactive interfact without using the native tools to do so. This means that Soundsky was not built using an application that speeds up the prototyping process. My desire here was to make sure I understood what actually happens behind the scenes of a prototype, instead of letting a design application handle it for me. I personally feel that the more I understand the intricacies of a process, the better I can manipulate the time-saving tools to maximize effects and minimize headaches.

◉ OpenGL ES Shaders

My latest foray into graphics-related technology is writing shaders using GLSL ES. Shaders, programmable parts of the graphics pipeline, allow me to perform visual effects and transformations at a very low cost to program efficiency, since shaders are GPU operations. The choice to use GLSL ES comes from it's portablility and popularity in embdedded systems. Many of my shaders are meant for 2D graphics and effects, but my more recent shaders are focused on 3D visuals; depth-based blur, vertex-manipulated animation, shadow-mapping, and other related methods.

◉ MotherSea


MOTHERSEA is a short game made for the GMC Game Jam #6, built from scratch within 72 hours. Awarded 2nd Place and 'Best Presentation,' MotherSea is about a small fish & squid duo on the search for fish that have lost their way in the darkness of the deep ocean. The fish can control light orbs found around the map for navigation, and the squid uses ink to propel itself and attack.

Ink-based attack!

This game was my first serious attempt to use visual design & shaders to create an effect that would influence the gameplay itself, instead of simply looking pretty. The effect works by rendering the shadowing using alpha blending and passing that render to a shader which rounds each given pixel's colour to one of 5 colours in a pre-determined pallete based upon luminescence. The result is a visual design that is minimalistic and smooth while still being readable and functional enough to play.
